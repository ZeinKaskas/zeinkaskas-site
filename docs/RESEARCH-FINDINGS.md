# PART 6: VSL RESEARCH (B2B FOCUSED)

## Optimal B2B VSL Structure
Based on research, the best B2B VSL for high-ticket consulting:

### Length: 5-8 minutes (not 2 min like B2C, not 20 min like webinar)
- Under 10 minutes is the sweet spot for B2B
- Long enough to build the case, short enough to respect busy executives' time

### Recommended Framework: Problem-Proof-Offer (PPO) + Cost of Inaction

**Section 1: HOOK (0:00-0:30)**
- Pattern interrupt. NOT "Hi I'm Zein." Start with a bold claim or provocative question.
- Example: "Most companies are spending 6 months and $200K on video content that AI can produce in 5 days."

**Section 2: PROBLEM + COST OF INACTION (0:30-2:00)**
- Define the pain: companies know AI is changing everything but don't know how to implement
- Cost of Inaction: 40-60% of B2B deals are lost to inaction, not competitors
- Make them feel the cost of doing nothing
- Use their language: "Your competitors are already using AI to produce content at 10x speed"

**Section 3: PROOF (2:00-4:00)**
- This is where CLIENT INTERVIEWS go
- 3-4 short clips (15-30 seconds each):
  - Client 1: "How was working with Zein?"
  - Client 2: "What results did you see?"
  - AI founder: "Why Zein understands this space"
  - Colleague: "What makes Zein different"
- Intercut with results: Burjeel 8K video, PLAAY renders, Health Lottery work
- Credential flash: Bestseller, Puig, Northern & Shell logos

**Section 4: WHAT I DO (4:00-5:30)**
- Not a feature list. Transformation language.
- "I don't build tools. I build systems that replace months of production with days."
- Services in plain language: AI consulting, AI video production, workflow automation, team training
- Quick case study montage (3-5 seconds each)

**Section 5: CTA (5:30-6:30)**
- Clear, singular CTA: "Book a 15-minute discovery call"
- No pressure. "If what I just showed you is relevant to your business, let's talk."
- Secondary: "Or email me at hello@zeinkaskas.com"
- Value-first: "In 15 minutes I'll tell you exactly where AI can save you time and money in your workflow"

### Urgency Techniques for B2B (NOT cheesy)
1. **Cost of Inaction** - "Every month you wait, your competitors are producing more content for less"
2. **Market timing** - "AI production tools are advancing every month. Early movers get 12-18 months of competitive advantage"
3. **Capacity scarcity** - "I work with 3-4 clients at a time" (genuine, not manufactured)
4. **Social proof acceleration** - "My last 3 clients came from referrals within 2 weeks"

### Testimonial Interview Structure
Questions for client interviews:
1. "What was the challenge you were facing before we worked together?"
2. "What was it like working with me?" / "How would you describe the experience?"
3. "What specific results or outcomes did you see?"
4. "What surprised you about the process?"
5. "What would you say to someone considering working with me?"
6. "Were there any weaknesses or things you'd change?" (shows authenticity)

For AI industry founders:
1. "How do you see AI changing creative production?"
2. "What separates someone who understands AI systems from someone who just uses AI tools?"
3. "What's your experience working with / knowing Zein?"
