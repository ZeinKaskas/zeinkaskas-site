## What we did (Zein's notes)

We did a shoot for PepsiCo that delivered creative across more than one of their subsidiary brands. The page should read as one PepsiCo engagement, with Quaker and Cheetos as the brands within it.

The crunch was timing. PepsiCo had a shoot coming up and didn't have the runway to build the sets they wanted. Building a real set eats a chunk of the production schedule even when everything goes right, and they didn't have that kind of time.

So instead of forking out for the build and waiting for it, we generated the backgrounds in AI. Same kind of approach as an LED-volume production where the world behind the product is virtual instead of physical. Quality looked the same as a built set, but we could deliver three different worlds (Day, Night, Sunset) for Quaker instead of one, and we could deliver them on the timeline they actually had.

There was no compromise on look. The brand showed up cleanly. The product still felt right. They just didn't have to build anything.

Cheetos was part of the same engagement but the assets and notes for that side are still incoming.

## Why this is worth a post

It's the cleanest possible flex on the parent-brand level. PepsiCo is one of the most recognized brand owners on the planet. Shooting for multiple of their subsidiary brands in a single engagement is a real signal of trust and capability. The post can show the breadth (multiple brands) and the depth (the LED-substitute approach on Quaker) at the same time.

It also opens the door to a virtual-set conversation that's familiar to higher-end brand teams. We can compare what we did to the way Mandalorian-style productions work, except faster to spin up and more flexible per shot.

## The framing

Lead with the engagement, not the cost. The takeaway: same set quality, multiple time-of-day looks for Quaker, ready on a timeline that wouldn't have allowed a built set at all. Do not mention savings. Lead with timeline and flexibility.
