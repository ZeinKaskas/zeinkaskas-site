## What we did (Zein's notes)

The Meena project was supposed to be a two-week build. Two weeks is already tight for what they wanted, but two weeks is what we had.

Then unforeseen circumstances knocked it down to one week.

Then halfway through that one week, Nano Banana Pro stopped working. Globally. There was a downstream error on Google's side that took everyone in the AI creative space offline for three or four days. We couldn't generate any client work, not just for Meena, for anyone. The whole industry was on pause.

By the time it came back up, we had basically zero time left. So we got the whole team in one room and finished the project in a single 30-hour sitting.

The video shipped. It looked good. The client was happy.

## Why this is worth a post

Two layers stacked.

**Layer one, the rescue story.** Two weeks became one week became 30 hours, with a global model outage in the middle. The video exists because we were using AI. A traditional shoot of this scope on this timeline would have been impossible. The deposit would have been refunded, the relationship damaged, the spot dead.

**Layer two, the production craft.** A multi-generation cast and two eras of location, all pulled together inside the Consistent Characters workflow. The page can show the locked references and let the reader picture the spot the references built into.

## The framing

Open with the timeline squeeze. Two weeks. One week. Thirty hours. Nano Banana down. Then "and the video shipped." Let the reader sit with the impossibility for a beat.

Then the curtain pulls back: here's the cast, here's the world, here's how the system held them stable across every shot.

Close with the takeaway. AI did not make this faster than a traditional shoot. AI made this possible at all. That's the actual argument.
