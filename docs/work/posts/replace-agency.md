## What this is (from the old site, in your own words)

The post is a walk-through of the AI Campaign Engine. The version of this you wrote up before went something like:

> "AI Campaign Engine, Replace the Agency. A full mix of Lo Fi and Hi Fi ads in one go. Up to 30 assets per run."

That's the headline. Below it, the pipeline runs in five phases.

### Phase 1, Input Once, Understand Everything

We ingest your entire brand DNA, product details, audience data, visual guidelines, into the system. That becomes the source of truth so every generated asset is on brand by default, not by accident.

### Phase 2, Automated Creative Direction

The system takes simple "seeds" and expands them into full creative briefs. Every concept gets split into two paths:

- **Lo Fi.** Native, handheld, UGC-style.
- **Hi Fi.** Polished, cinematic, designed.

That single split covers most of the creative spectrum a campaign needs.

### Phase 3, Briefs to Assets

Two parts:

- **From Angles to Briefs.** Concepts become concrete deliverables across every aspect ratio.
- **Script to Shot.** Video sequences get sketched out so visuals are narrative, not random.

### Phase 4, Meta Optimized Generation

This is the part most "AI ad" tools skip. Three layers:

1. **Precise prompting.** Not "make me an image." We generate complex, meta-optimized prompts that control lighting, lens choice, film stock, and mood, so brand alignment is engineered in.
2. **Batch processing.** We don't generate one image, we generate hundreds. The system iterates on the prompt to find the perfect shot.
3. **Campaign-ready output.** The best assets get curated into a final library, ready to deploy across social, web, and print.

### Phase 5, Test Fast, Learn Fast

The whole point of running 30 assets in one go.

- **Scale testing.** Spin up dozens of variations in hours.
- **Validation.** Test demand before production.
- **Pitch ready.** Full campaigns sitting in your decks, not just one or two hero shots.

## Why this is worth a post

The headline ("Replace the Agency") is provocative on purpose. The post has to back it up with the actual pipeline, not vibes. The five-phase breakdown is what does the work, because it shows the parts of "agency" that get automated and the parts that don't.

The assets we have (1 product shot, 6 static posts, 6 video posts) map cleanly onto the pipeline output: a single source product, expanded into Lo Fi + Hi Fi static, plus video. That's the proof.
