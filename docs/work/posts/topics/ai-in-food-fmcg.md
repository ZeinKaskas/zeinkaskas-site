## What this post is

A practical look at where AI is actually working in food and FMCG right now. Real use cases, real shipped work.

## The use cases

**Food shots, hero and lifestyle.** The stuff you'd normally fly in a food stylist for. Hero shots, bite shots, billboards, lifestyle eating. The Food to Food shot folder has examples of all of these. Cinematic, not stock.

**Packaging mockups, 2D to 3D.** A flat label or 2D pack design becomes a photoreal 3D render. On a counter. In a hand. On a shelf next to competitors. Before you've even printed the first physical pack. The 2D-to-3D folder shows the whole flow: source 2D, the rendered 3D, a holding shot, an on-shelf shot.

**Pre-launch marketing.** Bambuyu and PLAAY both did this. Start advertising before the product is finalized. Test the brand world. Warm up the audience. Run real creative against real impressions before manufacturing locks in.

**Set and background design.** Quaker is the example here. Different backgrounds for the same product, day, night, sunset, all without building a set. The product stays consistent, the world around it changes. Every campaign brief becomes possible.

**Ad creative at variation scale.** Same hero product, dozens of contexts, dozens of audiences, dozens of cuts. Test fast, learn fast, iterate fast.

**Localized creative for different markets.** One product, many regional contexts. Different cuisines on the table around it, different rooms, different cultural cues. Stop shooting one campaign per region.

**Texture and ingredient stories.** Macro shots of crunch, drip, splash, melt. The shots that food brands always want and never quite get because they're expensive and the food keeps melting under the lights.

## The framing

Food and FMCG marketers have been told for years that AI will "replace your photographer." That's the wrong sales pitch and it doesn't convert.

The real story: AI lets you make imagery a camera literally cannot make on the timeline you actually have. A new packaging variant on shelf today. A product render before the product exists. The same hero shot in three different times of day in a single afternoon. A campaign ready before manufacturing finishes.

That's not cheaper. That's faster, freer, and it puts the brand team back in the driver's seat instead of waiting on a calendar.

The "fake cheese" objection has expired. The remaining objection is taste, and that's the part we don't compromise on.
