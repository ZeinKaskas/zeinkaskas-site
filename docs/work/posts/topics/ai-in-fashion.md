## What this post is

A practical look at where AI is actually working in fashion right now. Not "the future of fashion." The things you can run this quarter.

## The use cases

These are the buckets that come up over and over again. Each one is a real problem we solve, not a tech demo.

**Virtual try-on.** Same garment on multiple models. Same model in multiple garments. The classic. Where most fashion teams start with AI because the value is immediate: you stop needing a fitting day and a photo studio every time you want to see something on a body.

**Editorial generation.** A product flat (just the garment, no model) becomes an on-model editorial shot. Studio, street, beach, evening, whatever. The garment stays the hero, the world around it gets built.

**Model variation at zero marginal cost.** Body types, heights, ages, skin tones. The stuff that would cost a brand a six-figure casting day plus shoot day plus retouching. We do it from a single brief. This is the most underrated use case for inclusive brands.

**Fast SKU testing.** Want to see how a new colorway looks before you commit to dyeing 10,000 units? Generate it. See if the team likes it. See if customers do. The product team and the creative team can move at the same speed for once.

**Lookbook generation.** A full seasonal collection presented in a coherent visual world. Days, not weeks.

**E-commerce PDP imagery.** Multiple angles, multiple contexts, multiple lifestyle shots per SKU. The kind of completeness that makes a product page convert, without flying a crew somewhere.

**Color and material variants.** One base shoot, many colorways. Same model, same pose, same lighting. Just the fabric flips. This is real workflow magic.

**Campaign ads at variation scale.** Same garment, different audiences, different contexts, different creative angles. You can finally A/B test without going broke on reshoots.

## The framing

The wrong way to sell this: "AI is cheaper than a photo shoot." Brands that buy on price will get burned by audiences who can sniff out cheap AI from a mile away.

The right way: "AI lets us do things a camera can't." Pre-launch imagery for an unfinished product. A model that doesn't physically exist but represents your customer perfectly. Shot variations no shoot day budget could afford. That's the real argument.
