## What we did (Zein's notes)

Bambuyu wanted renders instead of a traditional photo shoot. The catch was their product images weren't final yet. So we edited the images and then rendered everything from there. That whole loop, edit then render, is something traditional product photography literally cannot do. You can't shoot a product that doesn't exist in its final form yet.

We did. Their products have now launched.

## Why this is worth a post

This is the strongest argument for AI product photography there is, and most people miss it. Everyone leads with "it's faster" or "it's cheaper." The actual story is workflow: you don't need the final product to make the final imagery. You can iterate on the product and the imagery in parallel, instead of having to wait for one before you can start the other.

That's a real change in how a product launch can work. Bambuyu got to start advertising and showing the brand world before they had final units. By the time the product launched, the marketing engine was already warm.
