## The signature insights (use these, they're memorable)

1. **"AI loves to go to the median."** It defaults to what its training data liked. You have to *force* it into taste, framing, lighting, color that's actually interesting.
2. **"The best place to learn AI tools is film school."** Storytelling first, tools second. Without the base craft, you make slop.
3. **"AI content won't convert if you use it to cut costs."** Audiences feel disingenuous. They can tell the difference between AI-as-tool and AI-as-shortcut.
4. **"You can't solo a good film."** Even AI films need the human-in-the-loop, the bouncing of ideas, the taste of multiple people. Community-of-makers is part of the work.
5. **"It's like gambling."** Prompt + references in, pray, spin. Honesty about how the tool feels, disarming, not corporate.
6. **Pre-production is the moat.** References, elements, folder structure, project context. Most people skip this; it's where the quality comes from.
7. **Sound design makes AI visuals come to life.** Lighting + color + sound is what separates "tech demo" from "production."
8. **Agents are the most underrated part of the stack.** Automate everything except the taste.

## Voice / tone rules for site copy

- **First-person plural** ("we") for production work.
- **First-person singular** ("I") for founder content / origin.
- **Confident, not corporate.** Direct, sometimes blunt ("AI loves the median," "you eat shit," "it's like gambling").
- **Honest about the hard parts.** The hard truths section is positioning gold, admit AI is gambling, admit slop is everywhere, admit cutting costs with AI doesn't convert. That honesty separates us.
- **No hype words.** Don't say "revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-generation." Zein doesn't talk that way.
- **Show, don't argue.** The work is the argument.
